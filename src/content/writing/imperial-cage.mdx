My boots hit the icy ground of Charros with a thud that reverberates through my body. The drop ship roars back into the sky, leaving Fulminata Company’s 5th Platoon in a swirl of frosty air. “Synchronize visors. Night vision on,” I bark into the comm.

Sergeant Lorenzo acknowledges with a short, “Roger, Lieutenant.”

The entrance to the terrorist compound looms ahead, camouflaged against the frozen landscape. I hate the cold. It seeps through my armor, a constant, gnawing distraction.

I tap my helmet twice, signaling for breachers to set explosive charges on the compound’s gates. The charges’ low hum pierces through the whistling wind as they reach capacity before a controlled explosion rattles the door off its hinges.

We move in. Inside, walls of ice loom above us, and every footstep crunches. An armed guard appears from a corridor. Before he can raise his weapon, three shots echo. He drops.

“Area clear,” Lorenzo reports, a hint of pride in his voice.

A fleeting shadow catches my eye at the far end of a corridor. “Targets ahead,” I murmur. We advance, bursting into a room filled with data servers and complex communication setups—a hub of strategic importance.

Suddenly, the lights go out. Darkness swallows my squad, and I hear the click of helmets trying to light the way.

“Hold positions,” I order, straining to hear over the humming servers. Then, distantly, I hear faint crackling, followed by desperate shouting. As I listen to the clamoring, I make out a heavily accented cry.

“Help us!”

The crackling continues with the shouting, and I realize the Platoon isn’t the only force hunting terrorists. I smack my hand twice over my chest, signaling the others to follow me. The cries for help are silent now, and the thick smell of ozone fills my nostrils. I still can’t see, but the rhythm of my fellow soldiers tells me all I need to know. The sound of boots stepping over each other; stepping over something.

With a loud beep, the bunker’s lights turn on again, and two soldiers run to us from the server room.

“Sir, we fixed the lights—” starts one of them, before seeing the gruesome sight before them. Terrorists lay dead, their bodies twisted in pain and fear. Burns snake up their skin like spiderwebs, and their faces contort in pain.

We didn’t do this.

Without warning, an explosion erupts behind us. I’m thrown forward. As I hit the ground, a grenade clatters beside me. I jump, trying to pull myself back before it explodes, but I trip on one of the bodies. My vision fills with white while my chest explodes with searing pain. My ears fill with the blast, dulling to a deafening ring.

As I piece reality back together, the tepid oozing of smart gel begins to staunch the bleeding in my abdomen, but it can’t do much to numb the pain. I force myself to sit up, watching the chaotic scene unfold before me—Lorenzo and the squad unleash a barrage of return fire, foreign shouts filling the corridor.

I drag myself into the cover of my squadmates, where I see our target, Sian Juun. We’re this close to capturing a regional leader of the Shining Path, yet here she is, firing erratically. She puts up a stubborn fight, clearly trained and formidable. We trade fire, but she finally retreats into an escape tunnel.

“Secure the area, disarm the servers,” I croak, clutching my wounded abdomen.

The platoon gathers in this corridor, their silence whispering more than anyone can express. I step up, wobbly at first but quickly regain my composure. Sergeant Lorenzo is the first to break the silence.

“Orders, sir?”

I cough, weighing opportunities in my head. “Get all the data you can from the servers. Lorenzo, call for exfil. We’re done here.”

The rumble of descending dropships reverberates through the compound. One lands closer to the entrance, the other by the body pile Lorenzo’s men have formed. “5th Platoon, on me. Let’s move!” I yell, clutching my injured abdomen.

We board the first dropship, my boots locking into the magnetic floor. The crewman initiates the nanotech medical unit, which clamps around my abdomen. I feel a surge of warmth and slight pinching as the nanobots repair the tissue. In minutes, the pain subsides; I’m functional again.

The dropship takes off, ascending into Charros’ atmosphere, blending into the blackness of space. Ahead, the Perseverance waits—our sanctuary and prison.

We approach her docking bay, which lies in shadow. The doors slide open, allowing the dropship to land inside. As we disembark, technicians scramble to remove the bodies from the second ship and place them in storage. The thought of those injuries makes me wince.

Lorenzo and I exit the hangar into a hall. I’m still processing what happened when Lorenzo asks, “What happens next?”

“We’ll have to debrief. They are not going to like anything we tell them.”

“Alright,” Lorenzo replies. “Let’s hope this doesn’t blow up.”

As we head to the Combat Information Center, I recognize a familiar technician hunched over a control panel.

“Avi,” I nudge, trying to get his attention. He doesn’t perk up, too engrossed in his work.

“Avitus!” I press with a raised voice.

He turns, his eyes widening with recognition. “Arik! Wow, you look like you took a beating.”

“Don’t I always?” I smirk, although it’s a painful expression. “What’s got you so tied up?”

“Experimental power loss protection. Cutting-edge, but very unstable.”

“We could have used that for this mission, Avi. Where were you?”

He chuckles. “Speaking of that. How’d it go on Charros?”

“We ran into some snags,” I reply. “but isn’t that always the case?”

“Well, if you need me, I’ll be here. This damn thing refuses to connect.”

I nod, making my way back through the hall.

“See you, Avi,” calls Lorenzo.

We walk into the Combat Information Center, a massive room with a curved screen stretching around the walls, displaying star maps and tactical overlays. Fulminata Company’s Captain, Veridus, stands with arms behind his back, scrutinizing data on the screen.

“First Lieutenant McLaren and Platoon Sergeant Lorenzo reporting, sir,” I announce, standing at attention.

“At ease,” he replies, dismissing formalities. “I reviewed your mission logs. Anything to add?”

I shake my head, “Nothing beyond what’s stated, sir.”

His eyes narrow. “We had actionable intel. You had a full platoon of our best. I hear reports of terrorists burned to death. You’re sure you have nothing to add?”

“We executed the mission to the best of our ability, sir. I do believe there was some kind of outside intervention, but that goes beyond my assigned scope.” I retort.

Veridus is about to respond when the room dims. A hologram flickers into life in the center: Praetor Althor, his face stern and his eyes sharp.

“Captain Veridus, Lieutenant Arik McLaren, and Sergeant Lorenzo: you are in the presence of Praetor Althor. Kneel,” commands the voice of the ship’s AI.

We kneel, a gesture I find increasingly grating when dealing with the Praetorate, but decorum demands it.

“Unexpected honor, Praetor,” Veridus mutters, stiffening.

“Honor? You allow a failure of this magnitude and talk of honor?” Althor snaps. “The operation on Charros was a failure. Sian Juun has escaped your grasp. Explain yourselves.”

“Sir, we were ambushed. We did our best under the circumstances,” Veridus starts, trying to defuse the situation.

Althor’s gaze locks on me. “And who allowed the platoon to be ambushed, Captain?”

I say nothing, trying not to worsen my dwindling chances for a peaceful revolution.

“Nevertheless,” Althor continues. “Your best is inadequate. The Praetorate talked, and we’ve concluded that this type of incompetence is borderline malicious.”

My disdain for the Althor bubbles up. “With all due respect, Praetor, this is absurd.”

“Is it?” Althor shoots back. “Or did you intentionally do nothing while perditors destroyed all possible assets? It seems to me you stumbled upon quite a bit of luck.”

How ludicrous of Althor to imply such a thing! “What are you accusing me of, Praetor?”

Althor’s scowl twists into a hint of a smile. “Treason, Lieutenant. For compromising Imperial security and deliberately impeding the goals of your Company.”

The room freezes. My pulse quickens. Treason? After all I’ve done for Imperium?

“Respectfully, Praetor,” cautions Veridus. “Does the Emperor know about these charges? I can’t imagine he’d be pleased to hear the Praetorate is charging one of our finest officers with treason.”

“He certainly knows full well, as do you, that the Praetorate must enforce justice,” Althor replies. “Prepare for an immediate jump to Natale and detain Lieutenant McLaren once you arrive. Your assignment is terminated.”

As the hologram dissipates, Sergeant Lorenzo looks at me, disbelief and concern flickering in his eyes. “This isn’t about Sian Juun. Althor is making a play to remove you.”

“Sergeant Lorenzo, you are out of line,” reprimands Captain Veridus. “It isn’t our place to defy the Praetorate.”

Lorenzo backtracks. “Apologies, sir. I didn’t mean to—”

Veridus pivots to me. “You’ll be formally arrested, McLaren,” he sighs. “If you want my advice, you should savor your last moments of freedom. We make the jump in 30 minutes.”

I stand there, stunned. Althor’s just ended my life as I know it.

I don’t know what to reply, apart from a simple “Yes sir,” as Lorenzo and I leave the room.

As we move through the ship’s corridors, personnel scurry about, strapping down equipment while others sprint to jump bays. People hastily look away when we notice them staring and whisper to each other when they think we’re out of earshot. By the time we pass Avi’s workstation, he’s already heard the news.

“You can’t let them do this, Arik!”

“I don’t have a choice,” I answer, my voice laced with resignation.

“There- well there has to be something we can do!” he protests.

“We’ll cross that bridge when we come to it, Avi.”

As Lorenzo and I hurry through to the jump bay, Avi calls out, “You’re already crossing the bridge, Arik!”

The viscous jump fluid binds to my skin as the final preparations for the jump begin. Muffled warnings propagate the ship as the pod I’m in slides into the rest of the bay. As I fully submerge in the fluid, I feel a well-expected prickle crawl up my spine as the Perseverance and her crew lurches forward, changing our speed and trajectory to match a route to Natale.

Then without warning, it happens. A deep, guttural hum emanates from the ship’s core, vibrating through the deck plates. My skin tingles with a strange charge, and I twitch involuntarily as the fluid works to restrain me.

And then, in a cosmic blink, we’re hurtling through space. My senses rebel, my perception of space and time melting away, leaving me in a kaleidoscopic landscape of swirling colors. Stray thoughts unfurl, and memories flood in. For the first time in years, I remember my parents—the patio, the dinner parties, the calm that filled our home. I was young, taken too early to remember their faces clearly, but the feelings remain. Those memories mesh and mutate, blending into the faces of my foster parents, who themselves morph into Imperial officers I’ve served under. I am, for a moment, free-floating in an incomprehensible ocean, detached from the restrictive frameworks of reality. Loyalty, duty, family—they all become indistinguishable.

Then, as abruptly as it started, the dissonance ends. The ship shudders slightly as we exit the slipstream. The fluid in my jump pod becomes less viscous, losing its adhesive qualities. It drains rapidly, freeing me from the cocoon that’s kept me in stasis during the jump. I emerge, shaking off the residue and the disjointed thoughts from moments ago.

“Arrival at Natale confirmed,” announces the ship’s AI. Armed guards wait as the pod opens. With Veridus overseeing, they slap restraints on me and lead me out. Our footsteps echo through the once-bustling hallways of the Perseverance as we reach the hangar.

Drop ships are already idling, waiting to take me to my fate. The guards direct me towards one, with Sergeant Lorenzo stepping up behind me. As we board the drop ship, a frantic “Wait!” reverberates through the hangar. I turn to see Avi racing to our dropship.

“I’m coming, too,” he pants.

“Specialist Quinn, you are out of place!” scolds Captain Veridus. “To run up here like that is worthy of a demerit!”

“Wait, please,” he begs. “Sergeant Encratis ordered me to go with you.”

Veridus swears. “Encratis was playing you, Quinn. He didn’t expect—” He sighs. “Fine, you were following orders. I’ll discipline Encratis soon enough.”

Upon landing at the Praetorate Citadel, the Praetorian Guard escorts us through sterile hallways lined with historical tributes to the Imperium’s past. Eventually, one guard leads me into a round chamber. He removes my restraints, only for me to don another set—these have sensor modules to ensure I don’t try anything during the trial.

“Wait a moment,” commands the guard. “The chamber will move up to the main floor when the Praetorate is ready. Until then, sit there.” He points at a chair in the corner. He exits the room, leaving me to ponder in the silence.

Then a buzzer sounds, and the floor starts sliding up. Here it comes—the blinding lights. I steel myself for my next fight, but then the floor stops. I look around, confused. Maybe they weren’t ready yet. Then the floor starts to recede down, and I stand up worried. Could Althor be making another play? Are there mercenaries waiting for me?

These thoughts whirl through my head as the floor speeds up, sliding me into the bowels of the Citadel. A dark tube quickly replaces the stark white walls of the chamber as I descend further. My ears start to pop from the change in pressure, yet I still sink until finally coming to a stop at the bottom. With the whistling of air no longer filling my ears, I make out a distinct droning. Evacuation alarms. In the Citadel, of all places!

A sudden click turns my attention to doors at the walls of the tube. I brace myself for an attack, clenching my fists. The door opens and my jaw drops.

“Hey, Arik!” smiles Avi, standing next to Lorenzo. I balk in disbelief.

“How did- why-” I sputter before Lorenzo grabs ahold of me.

“Arik, there’s no time to explain. We need to move, now.”

I oblige, and Avi pulls out a strange device. He places it on my restraints, and they disconnect and slide open. We sprint down the narrow corridor, our footsteps echoing on the metallic floor. Avi takes point, scanning a card at a security checkpoint that grants us access to a lift at the end of the hallway.

“We have five minutes before they lock down this section,” Avi warns, hitting the control panel to summon the lift. The lift doors open with a hiss, revealing a cramped space adorned with utilitarian fixtures. We step in, and Lorenzo hits the button for the sub-basement where the battlecruisers are docked.

As we descend, I turn to Avi. “How’d you know where to find me?”

“I hacked into the Citadel’s network when I heard the alarms. Took control of your chamber and rerouted it,” he explains.

The lift halts with a slight jolt and the doors open. We exit into a cavernous space filled with the imposing silhouettes of Imperial battlecruisers. One ship catches my eye—a sleek design with a dark, metallic finish.

“That’s our ticket out,” Avi nods towards the ship, as Captain Veridus and other members of Fulminata Company come into view, waiting beside it.

“That's Pandora,” Veridus says proudly. “We’re taking her to the Outskirts. It’s risky, uncharted since Emperor Tadius IV called them ‘beyond reach.’”. He looks at me, the unspoken tension palpable. “This is your only ticket out, McLaren.”

“I’ll take it,” I respond, craning my neck at the vertical beast. Avi starts interfacing with the ship’s control panel, while Lorenzo and Veridus guide the crew aboard.

Battlecruisers stand out from the rest of the Imperial fleet not only because of their unique shape but also their heightened autonomy that allows soldiers to make the most of their battery of weapons. As Pandora’s Engines whir to life and the ship begins to lift off, I look out of the top window to see the Praetorate guard rushing towards us. They fire their rifles at us, a desperate act of futility. Pandora rises further into Natale’s atmosphere, and the Imperium is helpless to stop us—after all, they wouldn’t dare attack one of their prized vehicles.

An urgent message appears on the communications screen, but we ignore it. Nothing good can come out of talking with the organization now hunting us down for theft and treason.

We strap into our seats as Fulminata Company bids farewell to Natale and Pandora initiates a specialized jump sequence. As the ship prepares, I can’t help but wonder: Are we explorers or are we fugitives? The answer, I suspect, hinges on what we find there. Then, finally, we jump.

The ship shudders slightly as it completes the jump. As I peer out the battlecruiser’s viewscreen, I see an expanse of space unlike any within the Imperium. Rust-colored planets, azure nebulae, and a web of irregular asteroids fill the vista.

“Begin scanning for local frequencies,” Veridus orders. “See if they’re broadcasting any sort of identification or warnings.”

After some tense moments, Avi chimes in. “I’ve detected several signals, but they’re definitely not Imperial. They’re broadcasting using... lasers.” He scoffs. “There’s no quantum encryption, either. This’ll be only a second.”

A heavily accented voice crackles through the speakers, speaking in a language that calls back to the Imperium’s ancient texts. “Transgrediendi on sovereign agrum. Identify yourselves and your intentions.”

A soldier breaks into laughter. “That’s old Qeixmian!”

Veridus, intrigued but tense, is about to order a reply when the ship’s warning systems blare to life. The screen displays three Outsider warcraft, weapons locked onto our position.

One of the crewmen attempts to ease the tension. “Sir, they’d hardly scratch our shields with those armaments.”

“But what if they have reinforcements we haven’t detected? Respond in Qeixmian, now,” Veridus commands.

Avi nods, tapping out a message. Once sent, we hold our collective breath. After what seems an eternity, the Outsiders reply. Skeptical but not hostile, they propose a meeting on neutral territory—an asteroid they’ve terraformed, a practice the Imperium had deemed obsolete centuries ago.

As preparations for disembarking begin, Sgt. Lorenzo corners me. “McLaren, you’re de facto second-in-command. What are you willing to sacrifice for our survival?”

His question catches me off guard. “I... I can’t answer that now, Lorenzo. Our mission right now is to clear our names.”

Lorenzo nods, evidently not fully satisfied, but he falls in line. “Understood, Lieutenant.”

As we navigate towards the agreed meeting point, Avi pauses abruptly. “Captain Veridus, Pandora’s received something you need to hear.”

Veridus leans in as Avi decodes the encrypted message, and Avi’s face tightens with urgency. “Captain, the Praetorate isn’t just seeking justice. They’re planning a full-scale annexation of the Outskirts.”

The weight of Avi’s revelation hangs in the air as we head towards the delegate. Outsider representatives in a diverse array of armor patterns and tribal insignia stand at attention. Some are visibly armed, while others stand without weapons. Each person has a different amalgamation of armor and symbols, a far cry from the unity of Imperial forces.

Captain Veridus takes a deep breath and initiates the conversation through our translation device. “We arrive on this ground with no intention of hostility. We come as refugees fleeing injustice, not as conquerors.”

A woman in the center scoffs. “It’s curious, isn’t it? For refugees to arrive armed to the teeth.”

Veridus pauses, weighing his words carefully. “Our circumstances are... complex. Suffice it to say, we had to make do with what we could commandeer. We do not aim our weapons at you or your people.”

The woman eyes Veridus and then shifts her tattooed gaze to the rest of us. “And who are ‘we’? You’ve yet to introduce your party.”

Veridus complies. “This is First Lieutenant Arik McLaren, our second-in-command, and Avitus Quinn, our communications specialist. Sergeant Lorenzo is our tactical expert. We are Fulminata Company, wrongfully accused and fugitives of the Great Imperium and its Praetorate.”

Whispers emanate throughout the Outsider delegation. They begin to step back slowly.

“You are with Imperium?” the woman asks cautiously.

“As exiles. We have no intentions to fight.”

She exchanges glances with her comrades before refocusing her attention on us.

“Very well. You will stay here, for now. If you try to move away, we will destroy you.”

Veridus nods. “Understood. Now, if you please,” he glances back at us. “We’ve introduced ourselves, but who are you?”

The woman steps up and stares down Veridus. “I am Primus Zyn, sworn leader of the Outside.”

To her right, a young man with a tattoo around his eye stamps the ground twice. “Sentinel Messenio!”

A timid woman to Zyn’s left walks up beside her. “I am Dicentis Servilia.”

We all bow our heads in acknowledgment. “We thank you for your hospitality.”

Before the delegation can respond, Avi’s handheld device vibrates urgently. His face loses color as he reads the incoming message. “Captain, you need to hear this,” he says, his voice tinged with dire urgency. “Multiple incoming signatures detected. It’s a Praetorate fleet.”

Almost on cue, the sky erupts in light as Praetorate ships burst through their jump points, immediately opening fire on the startled Outsider craft orbiting the asteroid. Trust evaporates. The Outsiders train their weapons on us in an instant. Our hands shoot up defensively.

Veridus wastes no time. “Primus Zyn, we did not know about this attack, but we can help. We are not part of this invasion,” he urges, pointing at Pandora. “Our cruiser, Pandora. She can help repel the attack.”

She hesitates, then orders the Outsiders to lower their weapons. “You will do no such thing.”

Zyn turns to one of the delegates. “You will keep these invaders in their cruiser. We will meet at the flagship. Any sign of defiance, you kill them.”

The guards push us back into Pandora, where we gather with the rest of the crew. Back in the command center, the tension has shifted. Lorenzo, previous reservations seemingly forgotten, is all business. “I can man the gunnery stations. These Praetorate ships won’t know what hit them.”

“We’ll have to wait,” I warn Lorenzo.

I signal an Outsider guard to watch the display. “Do you see those fighters? We can destroy them from here. The ship doesn’t need to move.”

The guard looks me in the eye and shakes his head. “Do not try such tricks.”

Avi beckons me aside, his console displaying scrambled communications data. “I found chatter about something called ‘Cosmic Renaissance.’ They’re also talking about Dicentis Servilia.”

The guard’s eyes shoot to Avi, a look of disbelief on his face. “Servilla? How could they know her? She has not spoken to anyone on the Inside until now!”

He grabs his communication device. “Primus! The invaders know about the Dicentis.”

No response. “Primus!” he cries again, only to be met by a far more sinister voice.

“We come not as conquerors, but as liberators. The Cosmic Renaissance is upon us—a new age of enlightenment, progress, and unity. You stand on the precipice of a new order.”

Althor, in all his narcissism! Avi starts looking through communication channels, but all are flooded with Althor’s slimy voice.

The guard turns his weapon on Veridus. “I’m taking control. You have great firepower, yes?”

Veridus grins. “Some of the most powerful.”

The guard nods. “Change of plans!” He yells into his communicator. “Let these people fight!”

Pandora detaches from the asteroid and begins to rise, towards the flagship.

“Gun stations are manned and operational,” confirms Veridus.

“Engage at will,” I instruct, my focus torn between the looming flagship and Avi, who is feverishly typing at a console.

A barrage of canon fire flies up from Pandora and strikes the flagship’s shields. Immediately, fighters turn their attention down to swarm us. Gunfire follows, shredding several fighter engines in their blasts.

“Arik!” shouts Avi. “Praetor Althor is at the helm of the flagship!”

I realize this is a chance to use Althor’s arrogance against him. “Prepare for boarding!” I order.

A burst of static interrupts, followed by Lorenzo’s voice: “Breaching pods are ready, sir.”

Avi and I gather troops into one of them and the hatch around the pod closes.

“Fire,” orders Veridus.

The pods launch, closing the distance in a blink. A moment of violent shaking, and we’re through the flagship’s hull. The doors open, and we move swiftly—Avi, Lorenzo, a platoon of our best, and me. We’re in the belly of Althor’s lair.

“Our progress is being broadcast live across the Imperium,” Avi whispers. “I’ve piggybacked on their propaganda feed.”

Several firefights and a maze of corridors later, we stand before a massive bulkhead—the command chamber. I gesture to Avi, who hacks the door controls. It slides open, revealing Praetor Althor, calmly watching his handiwork with his hands neatly behind his back.

“Mr. McLaren, you’re not improving your outlook. Breaching a capital ship, on top of high treason? You really should know better.”

“We both know I didn’t do what you accuse me of,” I retaliate. “And the deeper I dig, the more your name appears.”

Althor laughs and turns around. His sour face twists with a hint of amusement. “Is that so? And what would you expect to find?”

“You’ve made several impulsive moves lately,” I point out. “Invasion, arrest, lies. What are you after?”

Althor smirks. “You could say I’m a man of ambition. I see a future that’s different from the stagnation we’re wallowing in. A future that calls for new leadership.”

“And what of the emperor?” I push, watching his face for any sign of a tell. “Surely he wouldn’t want to engage in a costly war. Especially when fighting against internal terrorist groups.”

Althor’s smile fades into a scowl. “If you’re not part of the solution, you’re part of the problem. Sometimes you have to remove the rot to let something new grow.”

“Even if it involves subverting the Imperium? What about Sian Juun? Was her escape part of your ‘solution’?” I bait him, subtly, yet directly.

“Ah, Sian Juun. A small piece, yet such a useful one,” he muses. “She served her purpose to shake the tree, so to speak. To disrupt the comfort and start the wheels of change. Unlike you, a stickler for a broken system.”

I glance at Avi, who gives me a slight nod. We’ve got him. Althor hasn’t outright admitted to framing me, but he’s shown his corruption live in front of the Imperium. Now we need to see how these revelations play out in our final moves against him.

For once in a long while, I grin with satisfaction. “You may want to reconsider your agenda, Althor,” I say, pulling my sidearm and aiming it at him. “You’re live across the Imperium.”

Before Althor can react, Lorenzo and his platoon storm the command center, securing Althor and his staff. We waste no time. Before retreating to Pandora, send a high-priority, quantum-encrypted message to the Emperor’s flagship.

Within minutes, a colossal jump rupture forms, and the Emperor’s behemoth flagship Haven materializes. We can hear the displacement through space, a menacing whine. Its presence alone halts the ongoing battle, as even the Praetorate ships freeze, recognizing Haven’s authority.

The Emperor’s holographic image appears on Pandora’s bridge. He is a young man, hardly over the age of twenty. His very presence commands us to kneel before his stern face.

“Captain Veridus, Lieutenant McLaren. Your actions have not gone unnoticed. Bring Praetor Althor aboard Haven for immediate questioning.”

We comply, escorting Althor into the Emperor’s chamber aboard Haven. The tension is palpable. The Emperor’s dark green eyes meet Althor’s.

“Do you think all I do is sit here, tending to the flowers?” He asks. “That I simply live lavishly while the Praetorate does all the work?”

Althor scoffs. “I’ve always been loyal to the Imperium, my lord,” he starts before the Emperor slaps him across the face.

“Let me be clear,” Tadius V hisses. “You have no place dictating the actions of the whole. Your responsibilities were that of a cog. To reduce the burden on me.”

Althor’s face twists into a rage, but he can’t say anything.

The Emperor pulls back. “You have strayed far from the path.” He gestures towards the trees in the chamber. “Guards.”

Four guards emerge from the miniature forest and drag Althor out of the chamber, as he sputters.

“YOU CAN’T FATHOM WHAT YOU’VE DONE,” shrieks Althor. “THE IMPERIUM FLOURISHED UNDER MY AUTHORITY! YOU CAN’T DO THIS!”

His cries die down as the guards take him to his fate.

Despite the arrest of Althor, the invasion is far from over. The Emperor commands: “The campaign will continue. The Outskirts will be annexed, but not exploited. They too shall bask in the glory of the Imperium.”

It’s a bitter pill for Primus Zyn and the Outsiders, who fought valiantly to protect their home. As we depart, I can’t help but feel that this is an irreversible turning point for both the Imperium and the Outskirts.

After the Emperor’s grandstanding finishes and the fleet broadcasts his voice for the galaxy to hear, he turns to me.

“Lieutenant, your service to the Imperium never wavered, even in your time of accusation.” He looks down, slightly ashamed. “Despite the efforts of my Praetorate, you still built a plan of action. You escaped here, where you could regroup.”

“My lord, that wasn’t just me. I had help from my Company, from my Captain.” I note.

“All the better,” counters the Emperor. “I have been keen on you for some time. Your skills and prowess are some of the finest in the Imperium, but the military will choke you, eventually.”

I start to realize what the Emperor might want from me. “What are you asking, lord?”

“Governance.” He turns back to the rainbow of flowers. “We live in a time of turmoil. Thanks to your revelations, I know there aren’t many I can still trust.”

He arranges the flowers by color, plucking a violet rose and handing it to me. “These territories need leaders they can trust, Lieutenant. So, will you become Governor McLaren? Or perhaps a peacekeeper. We can ensure the prevalence of order and justice.”

I stand there, considering the Emperor’s offer. The weight of the decision is immense. Will I be the arm of an Empire I question, governing a territory I helped conquer? Or is this a unique opportunity to steer the course of the future, for both the Outskirts and the Imperium?

I look up to meet the Emperor’s gaze, the room awaiting my response.
